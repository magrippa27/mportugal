Amateur radio remains one of the most rewarding hobbies for those interested in communication technology. This guide will help you take your first steps into this exciting world.

## Licensing Requirements

The first step is obtaining your amateur radio license. The exam covers basic radio theory, regulations, and operating practices.

## Essential Equipment

Starting with amateur radio doesn't require expensive equipment. A basic handheld transceiver can get you on the air for under $50.

## Making Your First Contact

Your first radio contact is an unforgettable experience. Start with local repeaters to build confidence before venturing into HF bands.
